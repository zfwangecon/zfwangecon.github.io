I am currently a graduate student in Systems Science at the Beijing Normal University, where I attained Bachelor in Economics. Institute for the Study of Labor (IZA), and a Fellow at Global Labor Organization (GLO). I graduated with a PhD in Economics from Duke University in May 2015.

My research interests include systemic risk, the economics of education, urban economics, and machine learning applications. I have published papers in National Accounting Review, the Journal of Human Resources, the Journal of Political Economy Microeconomics, and the Journal of Econometrics. I am also an associate editor of the Annals of Economics and Statistics. I have taught courses on econometrics, data science, and economics of education at both undergraduate and graduate levels. My research and teaching has been recognized by the OU Dodge Family College of Arts & Sciences Irene Rothbaum Outstanding Assistant Professor Award, which I received in 2022. I am proficient in various coding languages such as Matlab, Stata, R, Julia, Bash, Git, and LaTeX. I also have advanced language skills in Japanese and beginner-level skills in Korean and German.
